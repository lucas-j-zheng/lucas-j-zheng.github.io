Research at Prof. George Konidaris' Intelligent Robot Lab on simultaneously learning both levels of the hierarchical RL stack — the policy-over-options that decides *which* skill to execute, and the intra-option policies that define *how* each skill behaves.

## Problem

Joint training of hierarchical policies is unstable: the high-level evaluates a moving target, and the low-level chases a non-stationary selection distribution — a co-adaptation problem that drives option collapse in methods like Option-Critic.

## Approach

We investigate methods for stable concurrent learning of:

- **Option selection policy ($\pi_\Omega$):** Learns which option to invoke in each state, treating options as temporally extended actions
- **Intra-option policies ($\pi_\omega$):** Each option learns its own internal policy over primitive actions, with learned initiation sets and given termination conditions
