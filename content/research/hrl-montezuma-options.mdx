Research at Prof. George Konidaris' Intelligent Robot Lab investigating hierarchical reinforcement learning approaches to Montezuma's Revenge using built-in (hardcoded) options.

## Approach

We use DQN as the base learner over a set of predefined skill primitives (options) that abstract away low-level control — navigating to keys, opening doors, climbing ladders. The agent learns a policy over these options rather than over primitive actions.

To address the sparse reward and hard exploration challenges inherent to Montezuma's Revenge, we investigate structured exploration strategies:

- **CFN (Count-Based Frequency Novelty):** Drives exploration by rewarding visits to novel state-option pairs, encouraging the agent to try underexplored skills in underexplored states
- **RMAX:** Assigns optimistic reward estimates to insufficiently visited state-option pairs, guaranteeing exploration of the full reachable state space under the options

## Research Questions

- How far can hardcoded options take you in Montezuma's Revenge when paired with strong exploration?
- Which exploration strategies are most effective at the option level vs. the primitive action level?
- Where do built-in options bottleneck performance — is it the option set itself or the policy learning over them?
